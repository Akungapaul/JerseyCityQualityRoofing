Weehawken's unique content differentiators:
- Dramatic waterfront-Heights divide: Weehawken is split between the waterfront (at sea level along the Hudson) and "The Heights" (atop the Palisades). These are essentially two different towns with completely different roofing needs.
- Lincoln Tunnel: the entrance/exit of the Lincoln Tunnel is in Weehawken. Traffic congestion on tunnel approach roads (Willow Avenue, Boulevard East, JFK Boulevard) makes crew access and material delivery timing critical.
- Neighborhoods: Waterfront (luxury condos, Port Imperial), The Heights (older residential, tree-lined streets), Lincoln Harbor (mixed-use), King's Bluff (upscale, cliff-edge)
- Building stock TWO MARKETS:
  - Waterfront: modern luxury condos and mixed-use towers (2005-2025 construction), glass facades, green roofs, modern membrane systems under warranty
  - The Heights: century-old Victorian homes, colonials, multi-family walkups with aging shingle and slate roofs
- Port Imperial: massive waterfront development along the Hudson River with ferry terminal. Commercial roofing for retail, restaurant, and hospitality buildings.
- Hamilton Park: the historic dueling ground (Burr-Hamilton duel), surrounded by some of the oldest homes in Weehawken
- Wind patterns: waterfront buildings get direct Hudson River wind + Manhattan wind tunnel effects. Heights buildings get west-facing wind up the Palisades slope. Different wind mitigation needed at each elevation.
- Cost factors: waterfront luxury condo work commands premium pricing (high-end finishes, HOA approval processes). Heights work is more standard residential pricing.
- Unique challenge: crews may serve both a $2M waterfront condo (modern roof system, HOA board approval) and a $400K Heights Victorian (slate restoration, historic character) in the same day.

CRITICAL DIFFERENTIATION FROM GUTTENBERG:
- Weehawken has TWO distinct zones (waterfront vs. Heights), Guttenberg is uniformly dense
- Weehawken has modern luxury construction (Port Imperial), Guttenberg is older
- Weehawken's Lincoln Tunnel traffic is a unique access constraint
- Weehawken residential includes Victorian homes, Guttenberg is almost all multi-family

For commercial services: Port Imperial waterfront commercial/retail, Lincoln Harbor mixed-use, Weehawken waterfront hotels, Boulevard East upscale properties.
